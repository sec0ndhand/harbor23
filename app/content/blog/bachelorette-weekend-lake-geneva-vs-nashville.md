## The Nashville default, and why Chicago brides are breaking it

For the last decade, the default Chicago bachelorette itinerary has been the same: book flights to Nashville, pile into a downtown hotel, pub crawl Broadway, wake up in a pedal tavern photo. It's fun. It's also **~$1,800 per person** by the time you add flights, hotel, drinks, dinners, and an Uber that somehow went to Franklin.

There's a better version of the same weekend 90 minutes from home. Here's why.

## Lake Geneva vs Nashville — the honest breakdown

| | Lake Geneva | Nashville |
|---|---|---|
| **Flights** | None — 90 min drive from Chicago | $250–450 per person |
| **Lodging** | Whole lake house, 4BR, sleeps 14 | Hotel (2 per room) or downtown Airbnb |
| **Bar scene** | Wine bars, cocktail lounges, lakefront patios | Broadway bachelorette strip |
| **Day activities** | Boat day, spa, winery, lake swim | Honky tonks, shopping, more honky tonks |
| **Cost per person** | ~$400–600/person for 3 nights | ~$1,500–2,000/person |
| **Matching-outfit ceiling** | Reasonable | Very high |

The pitch is simple: you get the same "whole-weekend-together" feeling, minus the airport chaos and the credit card bill.

## What a Lake Geneva bachelorette weekend actually looks like

### Friday — arrive, unpack, toast

The crew rolls in from Chicago in the late afternoon. You walk into a 4-bedroom house you have **to yourselves** — no shared hotel walls, no lobby small talk — and immediately claim beds, pop the first bottle, and head to the patio. Dinner is either at **Sopra Bistro** (you want the pasta) or **Oakfire** (pizza + cocktails). First-night plan: wine bar crawl in downtown Lake Geneva — there are four good ones within two walkable blocks.

### Saturday — spa morning, lake afternoon, dinner, dancing

The winning bachelorette Saturday has three chapters:

- **Morning:** Spa day at **Avani Spa** inside The Abbey Resort. Book massages as a group and linger at the pools.
- **Afternoon:** **Lake Geneva Winery** for a tasting, then back to the house for pool/lake time. Some groups rent a pontoon from **Gordy's Boat House** instead — same vibe, wetter.
- **Evening:** Private chef dinner at the house, or reservations at **Pier 290** on the water. End the night downtown or around the fire pit with cocktails.

### Sunday — brunch, photos, home

Brunch somewhere with a view (**Daddy Maxwell's** is the local favorite), group photos by the lake, then the easiest-ever "trip home" — everyone's in their own bed in Chicago by Sunday evening.
