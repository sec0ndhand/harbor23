## The golf trip problem every Chicago crew has

You want to play 36 holes across two days with a group of 8–12. You want it to actually feel like a **trip** — grilling, a fire pit, somewhere that's not a chain hotel — and you want it within driving distance so nobody has to expense flights. The answer most Chicago golfers don't know: **Lake Geneva, Wisconsin.** Ninety minutes from downtown, five top-tier courses within a 12-mile radius, and an entire resort-town economy built around group stays.

## The five courses worth flying past

### 1. Grand Geneva Resort — The Brute

A **championship 18** that earned its name — long, muscular, with forgiving fairways that still punish tee shots that drift. It's the one your lowest handicap will talk about for weeks. Walkable if your back is good, carted if not. ~8 miles from downtown Lake Geneva.

### 2. Grand Geneva Resort — The Highlands

The Brute's Scottish links-style sibling, redesigned by Bob Cupp and Pete Dye. Rolling terrain, fescue roughs, and the kind of visual drama you don't expect in southern Wisconsin. Play The Brute in the morning and The Highlands in the afternoon for the full Grand Geneva day.

### 3. Geneva National — 54 holes of Palmer, Trevino & Player

Three separate 18-hole courses, each designed by a golf legend: **Palmer**, **Player**, and **Trevino**. Most groups play the Palmer course for its signature lakefront holes. The clubhouse food is better than it has any right to be. ~6 miles from Harbor on 23rd.

### 4. Abbey Springs Golf Course

The under-the-radar favorite. Public course on Fontana-on-Geneva Lake with postcard views on at least 4 holes. Tight fairways, tree-lined — it rewards accuracy over power. ~4 miles out.

### 5. Hawk's View Golf Club

Dramatic elevation changes you don't expect in flat Wisconsin. Two courses (Como Crossings and Barn Hollow), both worth a round. The layout makes it feel like a different state. ~12 miles — the farthest but worth the drive.

## The weekend format that works

A proven 2-day format for Chicago groups:

**Friday**
- 2 PM: leave Chicago
- 3:30 PM: arrive, grab beers, rack the clubs
- 4:30 PM: 9 holes at Abbey Springs or Geneva National (pick a shorter course for the opener)
- 7 PM: cookout at the house, fire pit, cards

**Saturday**
- 9 AM: 18 at The Brute
- 1 PM: lunch in downtown Lake Geneva
- 2:30 PM: 18 at The Highlands
- 6 PM: dinner back at the house, fire pit, second wind
- 10 PM: questionable decisions around the pool table

**Sunday**
- 10 AM: 18 at Geneva National (Palmer)
- 2 PM: pack, drive, home by dinner

Total: 63 holes, 5 cooked meals, one fire pit night, zero hotel desks.

## FAQ

**How close are these courses to each other?** All five are within a 12-mile radius. You can play any two in one day without driving more than 20 minutes between them.

**Best months to book?** May through October. Courses open around mid-April and stay playable through late October in a good year. June weekends book out first — plan 2-3 months ahead.

**How many people fit at the house?** 14 guests, 12 beds plus a couch sleeper. Most golf groups are 8–12 and have plenty of room.
